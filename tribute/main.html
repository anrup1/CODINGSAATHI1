Stephen Hawking

1942-2018

Introduction

Stephen Hawking was a renowned physicist, cosmologist, and author. He was born on January 8, 1942 in Oxford, England and passed away on March 14, 2018 in Cambridge, England. Hawking was best known for his work on black holes and his bestselling book "A Brief History of Time".

Early Life and Education

Hawking was born into a family of thinkers. His father, Frank, was a medical researcher and his mother, Isobel, was a secretary. Hawking attended University College, Oxford where he studied physics and graduated with a first-class honors degree. He then went on to complete his PhD in cosmology at Trinity College, Cambridge.

Scientific Contributions

Hawking made many contributions to the field of physics and cosmology. He is perhaps best known for his work on black holes. He theorized that black holes emit radiation, which became known as Hawking radiation. He also made significant contributions to the study of the origins of the universe and the nature of time.

Personal Life

Hawking was diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, at the age of 21. The disease caused him to gradually lose control of his muscles and he eventually became almost completely paralyzed. Despite his physical challenges, Hawking continued to work and make scientific discoveries. He also had a sense of humor and was known for his appearances on television shows such as "The Simpsons" and "Star Trek: The Next Generation".

Legacy

Hawking's work has had a significant impact on our understanding of the universe and the nature of black holes. He was also an advocate for scientific research and the importance of education. Hawking will be remembered for his contributions to science and his ability to inspire people around the world to pursue their dreams.
